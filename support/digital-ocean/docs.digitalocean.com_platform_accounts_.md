On DigitalOcean, you manage your infrastructure and billing with [teams](https://docs.digitalocean.com/platform/teams/). Your personal account on DigitalOcean lets you manage your team membership and your name, sign-in method, and email subscriptions.

![](https://docs.digitalocean.com/images/icons/accounts.svg)

How to Manage DigitalOcean Personal Account Settings

The My Account page lets you view and edit your login method, 2FA, team membership, and email preferences.

![](https://docs.digitalocean.com/images/icons/accounts.svg)

How to Manage Two-Factor Authentication for DigitalOcean Accounts

Use 2FA to add an additional layer of security against unauthorized access to your DigitalOcean account.

![](https://docs.digitalocean.com/images/icons/teams.svg)

DigitalOcean Teams

Teams are how you manage your billing and infrastructure on DigitalOcean. You can work by yourself by remaining the only person on your team or collaborate by adding more people to teams you own.

![](https://docs.digitalocean.com/images/icons/accounts.svg)

How to Deactivate DigitalOcean Accounts and Purge Account Data

Deactivate an account to stop billing and prohibit future resource creation. Optionally purge your account data to permanently remove the account from DigitalOcean and prevent account reactivation.

## Latest Updates

### 5 November 2025

- The control panel now logs you out after 12 hours. It notifies you ten minutes and one minute before the timeout so you can log back in. If you have multiple tabs open, logging in again in one tab keeps every tab active.


### 27 September 2022

- Users who sign up for DigitalOcean through the [referral program](https://docs.digitalocean.com/platform/teams/how-to/refer-others/) now receive a $200 account credit, increased from $100.


### 28 January 2022

- To continue improving collaboration on DigitalOcean, we have begun incrementally converting existing customers’ personal accounts to [team accounts](https://docs.digitalocean.com/platform/teams/).


For more information, see [the full release notes](https://docs.digitalocean.com/release-notes/).
